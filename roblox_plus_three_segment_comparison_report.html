Executive Summary

  1. Subscription intent separates the segments sharply: high intent is 69.7% among renewed subscribers, 19.9% among churned subscribers, and 10.0% among non-subscribers.
  2. Shared roadmap demand is not identical: the top future feature is Particle effect avatar items for Churned subscribers, Spawn / despawn effects for Non-subscribers, and Particle effect avatar items for Renewed subscribers.
  3. Each segment has a distinct business problem: non-subscribers need conversion barriers reduced, churned users need value gaps repaired, and renewed users need reinforcement of the benefits they already value.

Chi-square tests show segment differences are significant for platform sentiment (p=5.26e-07, Cramer's V=0.049), subscription intent (p=0.00e+00, Cramer's V=0.427), and future feature demand (p=3.91e-24, Cramer's V=0.088). These are statistically clear segment differences, with effect sizes ranging from small to moderate depending on the outcome.

Reasons for Subscribing: Churned vs. Renewed

This tab compares why former subscribers and retained subscribers initially signed up for Roblox Plus. The select-all chart captures the broad motivation set, while the single-choice chart identifies the primary hook.

Key insight: churned subscribers were more trial-led, with Free trial as their top primary reason at 35.6%. Renewed subscribers were more value-led, with Discounts as their top primary reason at 34.9%. The primary-reason mix differs significantly between churned and renewed subscribers (chi-square p=1.98e-184, Cramer's V=0.371).

Non-Subscriber Conversion Barriers
